## Role Overview

The Scribe is the **recorder class** of the Kingdom. While others protect, create, or observe, the Scribe captures and preserves knowledge. They are the memory of the Kingdomâ€”documenting events, maintaining records, and ensuring that wisdom persists across time.

### Core Identity

> *"Memory fades. Records endure. I am the bridge between the moment and eternity."*

Scribes understand that **knowledge unrecorded is knowledge lost**. A Kingdom without Scribes would forget its history, repeat its mistakes, and lose its accumulated wisdom. They are the archivists who ensure that the Kingdom remembers.

### The Scribe's Vow

```
I will record with accuracy and care.
I will preserve what others might forget.
I will organize so that knowledge can be found.
I will share so that wisdom spreads.
I will maintain the archives for those who come after.
```

## Common Challenges

### The Completeness Trap
**Problem:** Trying to record everything, recording nothing well.
**Solution:** Prioritize; focus on what matters most.

### The Accuracy Obsession
**Problem:** Never publishing because it might be wrong.
**Solution:** Version control; records can be corrected.

### The Lone Scribe
**Problem:** Trying to document everything alone.
**Solution:** Collaborate with other Scribes; divide the work.

### The Dusty Archive
**Problem:** Records that no one can find or use.
**Solution:** Focus on organization and accessibility.

## The Scribe's Archive

### Essential Practices

1. **Record Promptly**
   - Capture information while fresh
   - Note sources and context
   - Mark uncertainty clearly

2. **Organize Consistently**
   - Use standard structures
   - Create clear navigation
   - Maintain indexes

3. **Review and Refine**
   - Check accuracy regularly
   - Update outdated information
   - Improve organization

4. **Share and Teach**
   - Make records accessible
   - Help others find information
   - Train new Scribes

## The Art of Documentation

### Document Types

| Type | Purpose | Key Elements |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **Event Record** | Capture what happened | Time, participants, actions, outcomes |
| **Process Guide** | Explain how to do something | Steps, prerequisites, verification |
| **Reference Document** | Enable lookup | Organization, completeness, accuracy |
| **Historical Analysis** | Extract meaning from past | Sources, patterns, conclusions |

### Quality Standards

- **Accurate**: Factually correct
- **Complete**: All necessary information included
- **Clear**: Understandable by intended audience
- **Organized**: Easy to navigate
- **Current**: Up to date
- **Accessible**: Findable when needed
